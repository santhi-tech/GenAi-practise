There are situations where it makes sense to round up a number 
(rounding up is when the approximated number is more than the actual 
number). For example, if a school has 732 people including students, 
teachers and staff: the principal might order 750 sweets instead of 
700 sweets.  
There are situations where it is better to round down (rounding down 
is when the approximated number is less than the actual number). For 
example, if the cost of an item is ₹470, the shopkeeper may say that the 
cost is around ₹450 instead of saying it is around ₹500.
